## 🧠 TL;DR: How it works under the hood

GBLIN targets a strict asset allocation:
* **45% cbBTC** (Coinbase Wrapped BTC)
* **45% WETH** (Ethereum)
* **10% USDC** (Stability Layer)

Unlike traditional index tokens that rely on centralized off-chain keepers to maintain their peg, GBLIN internalizes its entire logic. Everything happens synchronously on-chain.

### 🛡️ 1. The Crash Shield (Capital Preservation)
Crypto dumps happen fast. If the internal Chainlink oracles detect a sudden drawdown greater than `CRASH_THRESHOLD_BPS` (20%) from an asset's peak price, the Crash Shield activates. 

The protocol automatically slashes the bleeding asset's dynamic weight (`SLASH_MULTIPLIER`) and re-routes that weight to the healthy assets (primarily the USDC stability layer). It stops catching falling knives and waits for the dust to settle.

### ⚙️ 2. Internalized Chainlink Oracles
To eliminate asynchronous vulnerabilities and oracle manipulation, GBLIN directly integrates `AggregatorV3Interface`. Every mint, burn, and rebalance operation pulls the synchronous price in the exact same transaction. No delays. No front-running the oracle.

## 🤖 Searchers & MEV: How to get paid

*Attention MEV bots and arbitrageurs: this protocol is designed to pay you.*

GBLIN doesn't fight MEV; it weaponizes it to maintain protocol health. When market drift pushes the index off its 45/45/10 target, anyone can call the `incentivizedRebalance` function. 

**The Deal:** You execute the swap (WETH to Asset, or Asset to WETH) using the protocol's liquidity. In return, the protocol immediately pays out a flat reward of **0.0001 ETH** directly to the `msg.sender`.

Because we are built on Base, where gas fees are pennies, that 0.0001 ETH (approx ~$0.30) is pure net profit for your bot. You keep the peg tight, you take the spread, and the protocol pays your gas. It's a win-win.

## 💰 Real Yield: The Stability Fund

Every retail mint and burn carries a tiny 5 BPS (0.05%) `STABILITY_FEE`. This doesn't go to the founder. It goes straight into the protocol's **Stability Fund**.

This fund acts as the protocol's insurance policy and pays for the MEV rebalancing rewards. But what happens when volume is high and the fund gets too big?

**The Dynamic Reserve & Yield Distribution:**
The contract calculates a dynamic reserve requirement based on the total Protocol Owned Liquidity (min 0.05 ETH, max 2 ETH). 
If the Stability Fund exceeds this ceiling, and the `YIELD_INTERVAL` (7 days) has passed, the `distributeYield()` function becomes available. The excess ETH is permanently removed from the reserve and distributed to the ecosystem. Real yield generated by volume, not by printing inflationary tokens.

## 🐋 Institutional In-Kind Facility

Whales shouldn't pay slippage. For large-scale capital deployment, GBLIN features an In-Kind minting and redemption facility. 

If you want to mint 100 GBLIN, the `quoteMintInKind` function calculates the exact underlying amounts of cbBTC, WETH, and USDC required. You supply the raw assets, bypass Uniswap routing entirely, and get minted GBLIN at zero slippage. When you want out, you burn your GBLIN and receive the raw underlying assets back.

## 🔒 Security & Trust Assumptions

We believe in "Don't Trust, Verify".
* **No Pausability:** You can always redeem your underlying assets. 
* **Sequencer Checks:** The contract checks the Chainlink L2 Sequencer feed. If the Base sequencer goes down, mints/burns revert to protect against stale pricing exploits.
* **Spurious Dragon Optimized:** The V5 contract logic was ruthlessly deduplicated to fit within the EVM bytecode size limits without sacrificing features.
* **Limited Ownership:** The `owner` can propose new assets (with a strict 48-hour Timelock) and update oracle addresses if Chainlink deprecates them. The owner **cannot** freeze funds, mint arbitrary tokens, or change the fees.
